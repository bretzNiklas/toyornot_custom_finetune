# Label Audit 2026-03-26

## Batch summary

- `41` labeled tasks
- `40` usable

  - `20` paper sketches
  - `13` wall pieces

  - `3` other / unclear
  - `1` unusable

## Main findings

### 1. Medium drift is real

Paper sketches are being scored much harsher than wall pieces.

Mean scores:

- paper sketches:
  - legibility `6.70`
  - letter structure `4.55`
  - line quality `4.25`
  - composition `4.25`
  - originality `4.65`
- wall pieces:
  - legibility `7.15`
  - letter structure `6.92`
  - line quality `7.08`
  - composition `6.54`
  - originality `6.54`

Interpretation:

- some of this may be real dataset quality difference
- but the gap is large enough that paper work is probably being penalized for roughness more than walls are penalized for weak structure

### 2. Character-only entries do not fit the current rubric

Example:

- `5eb8f39a-363a-4624-bee7-ab7cfc437990.jpg`

It is a character-focused drawing, but the current rubric still forces:

- `legibility`
- `letter_structure`
- `line_quality`

That produces labels that are internally inconsistent for a non-letter piece.
Decision after audit: character-only pieces are out of scope for v1 and should be marked unusable.

### 3. Legibility vs structure is mostly behaving correctly

Example:

- `28bf635a-64c8-42cb-bd27-a59bc89e7179.jpg`

This is highly readable but weakly built, so:

- high `legibility`
- low `letter_structure`

is acceptable.

This is not necessarily an error.

### 4. Wildstyle can legitimately score low on legibility and high elsewhere

Example:

- `55e35dbe-87ef-44f7-8aa3-22b1ad0d4a20.jpg`

Very low `legibility` with high `composition`, `line_quality`, and `originality` is defensible for complex wildstyle.

This is not a rubric failure on its own.

## Recommended labeling adjustments

### Keep doing

- keep `medium` as a required label
- keep `confidence` honest
- keep `color_harmony = null` when color is not relevant

### Change immediately

1. For `character` pieces:
   - mark them unusable
   - use reason `Character-only / out of scope`

2. For paper sketches:
   - do not over-penalize for notebook context, unfinished fill, or casual presentation
   - score the visible design and control, not how polished the photo looks

3. For wall pieces:
   - do not give automatic high marks for spray polish alone
   - keep checking `letter_structure` separately from paint finish

## Next target

Continue labeling until:

- at least `120-150` total usable labels
- with strong coverage in both:
  - paper sketches
  - wall pieces

Then build anchor examples across both media.
